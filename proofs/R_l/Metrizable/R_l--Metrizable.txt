Because <a href="/proofs/R_l/2nd-Countable/R_l--2nd-Countable.html"> \( \mathbb{R}_l\) is not second-countable </a>, by contrapositive of  <a href="/theorems/Exercise-30-5.html"> \( \S \)30 exercise 5(b) </a> in Munkres, \( \mathbb{R}_l \) is not Lindelöf \(\lor \) \( \mathbb{R}_l \) is not metrizable.

We know that <a href="/proofs/R_l/Lindelof/R_l--Lindelof.html"> \( \mathbb{R}_l\) is Lindelöf </a>, so \( \mathbb{R}_l \) must not be metrizable.
