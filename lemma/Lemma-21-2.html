Lemma 21.2 (Sequence Lemma)

Let \(X\) be a topological space and let \(A \subseteq X\).

  1. (a)If there is a sequence of points of \(A\) converging to \(x\), then \(x \in \overline{A}\).
  2. (b)Conversely, if \(X\) is metrizable and \(x \in \overline{A}\), then there exists a sequence of points of \(A\) converging to \(x\).

Proof.

Comment:

For (b), the only thing used from metric is a countable neighborhood base at \(x\): the balls \( B(x, \frac{1}{n}) \).

Therefore exactly the same argument works in any first-countable space:

If \(X\) is first-countable and \( x \in \bar{A} \), then \( \exists \) a sequence \( (x_n) \subseteq A \) with \(x_n \to x\).
