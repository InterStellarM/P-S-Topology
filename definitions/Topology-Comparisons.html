Finer, Coarser, and Comparable Topologies

Definition: Suppose that \(\mathcal{T}\) and \(\mathcal{T}'\) are two topologies on a given set \(X\).

  1. If \(\mathcal{T}' \supseteq \mathcal{T}\), we say that \(\mathcal{T}'\) is finer than \(\mathcal{T}\). If \(\mathcal{T}'\) properly contains \(\mathcal{T}\) (i.e., \(\mathcal{T}' \supsetneq \mathcal{T}\)), we say that \(\mathcal{T}'\) is strictly finer than \(\mathcal{T}\).
  2. Equivalently, \(\mathcal{T}\) is coarser than \(\mathcal{T}'\), or strictly coarser in the respective situations.
  3. We say that \(\mathcal{T}\) and \(\mathcal{T}'\) are comparable if either \(\mathcal{T}' \supseteq \mathcal{T}\) or \(\mathcal{T} \supseteq \mathcal{T}'\).
