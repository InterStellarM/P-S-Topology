Lower Limit Topology \( \mathbb{R}_l \)

Definition: The lower limit topology on \(\mathbb{R}\) (denoted \(\mathbb{R}_l\)) is generated by the basis \( \mathcal{B}_l = \{ [a,b) : a,b \in \mathbb{R},\ a < b \} \).

\( \)

Comment:

For any \(a < b\), the standard-open interval \((a,b)\) is also open in \(\mathbb{R}_l\). 2 ways of proof are shown below:

\(\)

1) direct union argument.

Choose \( n_0 \in \mathbb{Z} \) such that \( n_0 > \frac{1}{b-a} \).

Claim: \( (a,b) = \bigcup_{n=n_0}^{\infty} [\,a+\tfrac{1}{n},\ b\,) \)

Proof of \(\subseteq\): If \(x \in [\,a+\tfrac{1}{n},\ b\,)\) for some \(n\), then \(a < x < b\).

Proof of \(\supseteq\): If \(x \in (a,b)\), let \(\delta = x-a > 0\). By the Archimedean property, pick \(n\) with \(1/n < \delta\) (equivalently \(n > 1/(x-a)\)). Then \(a+\tfrac{1}{n} < x < b\), so \(x \in [\,a+\tfrac{1}{n},\ b\,)\).

\(\)

Each set \([\,a+\tfrac{1}{n},\ b\,)\) is a basis element in \(\mathbb{R}_l\), so their union is open by the definition of topology. Therefore \((a,b)\) is open in \(\mathbb{R}_l\).

\(\)

2) pointwise neighborhood argument.

\( \forall \) \(x\in(a,b)\). Let \(\delta=\min\{(b-x)/2,(x-a)/2\}>0\).

Then the basic \(\mathbb{R}_l\)-neighborhood \([x, x+\delta)\) satisfies \([x,x+\delta)\subseteq (a,b)\).

Since every point of \((a,b)\) has such a basic neighborhood inside \((a,b)\), the set \((a,b)\) is open in \(\mathbb{R}_l\).

(As a side note: this shows every standard-open interval is \(\mathbb{R}_l\)-open, so the lower limit topology is strictly finer than the usual topology by Lemma 13.3.)
