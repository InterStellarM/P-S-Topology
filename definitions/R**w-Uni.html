\( \mathbb{R}^\omega \) with Uniform Topology

\( \mathbb{R}^\omega \) is the countable infinite product of \( \mathbb{R} \) with itself. \( \mathbb{R}^w \) is generated by the standard bounded metric \( \bar{d}(x, y) = \min \{ \left| x-y \right|, 1 \} \) on \( \mathbb{R} \)

Definition: Let \( \{ X_\alpha \}_{\alpha \in J} \) be an indexed family of topological spaces. Uniform topology on \( \prod_{\alpha \in J}{X_\alpha} \) is the metric topology generated by \( \tilde{d_u} ((x_\alpha), (y_\alpha)) = \sup_{\alpha \in J} [\tilde{d_\alpha} (x_\alpha, y_\alpha) ] \), where \( \tilde{d_\alpha} \) is a bounded metric in \( X_\alpha \).

\(\)

Definition: If \(X, d\) is a metric space, then there is a topology \( \mathcal{T}_d \) called metric topology whose basis is a collection of "open balls" \( B_d(x, \varepsilon) = \{ y \in X : d(x, y) < \varepsilon \} \) for \( x \in X \), \( \varepsilon > 0 \).
