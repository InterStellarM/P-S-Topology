Convergence of Sequences

In an arbitrary topological space, one says that a sequence \(x_1,x_2,\ldots\) of points of the space \(X\) converges to the point \(x\) of \(X\) provided that, corresponding to each neighborhood \(U\) of \(x\), there is a positive integer \(N\) such that \(x_n \in U\) for all \(n \ge N\).

In \(\mathbb{R}\) and \(\mathbb{R}^2\), a sequence cannot converge to more than one point, but in an arbitrary space, it can.
